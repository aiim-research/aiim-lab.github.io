Invited talk AI-GAP 2023 - Algorithmic Biases in Artificial Intelligence from Interdisciplinary Perspectives

November 15, 2023

L’Aquila, Italy — AI-GAP 2023 - Algorithmic Biases in Artificial Intelligence from Interdisciplinary Perspectives, is thrilled to announce the participation of a renowned expert in the field of Algorithmic Bias and Fairness, G. Stilo, who has been invited as speaker for this prestigious event.

The workshop is set to take place on November 20-22, 2023, and promises to be an enlightening experience for all attendees. Stilo’s keynote address, titled “Uncovering Gender Bias in Academia” will be a focal point of the event. This keynote promises to shed light on an emerging and crucial aspect on Gender Bias in Academia.

Gender bias in education has gained considerable attention in recent years, as it carries profound implications for the academic community. However, while the problem has been tackled from a student perspective, research is still lacking from an academic point of view.

This presentation aims to bridge this research gap by shedding light on the complex dynamics of gender bias within the Software Engineering (SE) community. We first present a literature review on the subject, assessing the current trends in the literature.

We then move on to an in-depth analysis, focusing on two main topics:

  • With Network Analysis techniques, we infer prevalent patterns in the social behaviors of the SE community on both a global and Italian scale.
  • Employing a formal bias metric, we assess the extent of gender bias in career advancement within the Italian Informatics and SE communities.

Through our findings, we derive insightful conclusions regarding the current state of gender bias within the SE field, highlighting critical areas where further research and intervention are essential.
